I'm a Research Software Engineer at IBM Research in Zurich, Switzerland. I am broadly interested in geometric deep learning and graph-based reasoning for large multimodal models.

Before joining IBM Research I completed my BSc and MSc degrees at ETH Zurich, both in Information Technology and Electrical Engineering.

Publications

Provably Powerful Graph Neural Networks for Directed Multigraphs

AAAI 2024 [oral]

Béni Egressy, Luc von Niederhäusern, Jovan Blanusa, Erik Altman, Roger Wattenhofer, Kubilay Atasu

We show that with a few simple adaptations to your favorite GNN architecture, you can detect any directed subgraph pattern. This leads to significant improvements in detecting financial fraud in large-scale transaction networks.

Realistic synthetic financial transactions for anti-money laundering models

NeurIPS 2023

Erik Altman, Jovan Blanuša, Luc von Niederhäusern, Béni Egressy, Andreea Anghel, Kubilay Atasu

We present AMLworld, a synthetic transaction generator that can realistically model money laundering. It does so on a very large scale (comparable to large international banks) and thus enables benchmarking models on realistic data volumes.
